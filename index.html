Aerospace Engineering

What does an Aerospace Engineer do?

According to the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics, an Aerospace Engineer designs, develops, and tests, aircrafts, spacecraft, satellites, and missiles. The median American pay for an Aerospace Engineering was $126,880 with a job-growth of 6%. In Canada, the average Salary is roughly $117,250.

Example Job | Canadian Armed Forces

Aerospace Engineering Officers are responsible for all aspects of the engineering, maintenance and management of military aircraft; and all of their support equipment and facilities during military operations.

Example Job | Actalent

As an Aerospace Design Engineer, you'll provide support on an exciting aircraft modification project; a cargo conversion project. You will be part of the Aircraft Structures team and will supporting the development of technical design solutions for aircraft primary structures and components such as the frame, skin, intercostal, ribs, doubler, etc.

Undergraduate Program

University of Toronto: St. George Campus

The University of Toronto is one of the most renowned universities within Canada. The program starts with two years of general engineering and then 2 more years of specialization in Aerospace Engineering. Furthermore, the benefit of UofT is the vast amount of graduate programs that includes 3 graduate programs related to Aerospace.

Credential

Through the completion of Engineering Science, students are rewarded with a Bachelor of Applied Science in Engineering Science. On your transcript, your major will be listed (Aerospace Engineering, Robotics, or other major in Engineering Science). No certifications are needed within Canada, but a PE or Professional License is recommended.

Application Process

1. Review

Review the admission requirements, namely being grade requirements and courses.

Learn More

2. Visit OUAC

Visit the Ontario Universities' Application Centre or OUAC and complete the application using your Ontario Education Number of OEN. To be eligible for decisions in February, apply before the early consideration deadline. Students who rank UofT Engineering below 3rd choice will be disadvantaged in the admission process. Recommend applying before November 7th. Students who want to apply to UofT Engineering are required to pay an assessment fee of $42.00 when performing the OUAC application.

Learn More

3. Application Portal

After receiving the application, UofT will send a JOINid that needs to be enabled. Complete the mandatory Online Student Profile in the Engineering Applicant Portal. The Online Student Profile consists of sections including: Engineering Program Choice, Academic Timeline, Academic Profile, Extracurricular Activities, Personal Profiles. Complete the Online Student Profile by December 1st to make the February decisions. Submit any documents related to high school education outside of Ontario. Provide proof of English Language Requirement if necessary.

Learn More

4. Optional Applications

Apply for any scholarships or financial aid (domestic, University of Toronto Advance Planning for Students (UTAPS), International Study Permit). Apply for residence if wanted before March 31st. Track the status of the application within the Engineering Applicant Portal.

Barriers

Finance and Travel

Due to the University of Toronto being very prestigious school internationally, naturally costs are quite high. On top of that, the St. George Campus is decently far from me and would require long busing and transportation times and costs. It will be unlikely that I will have a car at the time and finally, my grandmother will be alone for longer, which could be dangerous.

Competitiveness

Before realizing my slight oversight, I thought the program would only require high 80 marks and would be relatively safe path option for me. Instead, EngSci is one of the most difficult undergraduate programs in Canada, roughly ranking 4th. On top of that, the listed admission grade listed is low 90's, thus supporting students with averages higher than that.

Strategies

Finance and Travel

The simplest method for reducing finance costs is applying for a scholarship. All universities offer small scholarships simply for admission and larger funds for stronger applicants. On top of that, continue to save money for university as I already have an account for it. In regards to transportation, see if carpooling, obtaining my own car, or reesidence is an option. This allows me to travel back and forth a bit faster, but still leaves the issue of my grandmother being alone for longer.

Competitiveness

Try to build a strong portfolio and resume. Seek out volunteer, work, and lab experience as all help reinforce many skills such as analysis, responsibility, curiosity, teamwork and more. Continue to build and specialize in different extracurricular activites. Finally, compete in various competitions that revolve around my interests. By going in depth about different interests I have (related to Aerospace if possible), I can help my chances to be admitted.
